Baking and Pastry Arts

The baking and pastry arts industry is a specialization within the category of food preparation and service. Although this fast-paced industry can be competitive, high turnover can create room for ambitious budding chefs. Read on to learn about education and career options in this field..

Inside Baking and Pastry Arts

The field of baking and pastry arts is a small part of the larger industry of food preparation. As in many industries, it helps to have prior experience in the field to ultimately become a successful pastry chef. Education, experience, on-the-job training and industry certifications are all important for obtaining higher-paying positions in the industry, such as those with 5-star restaurants, hotels, boutique bakeries or cruise ships. Chefs and head cooks must be detail oriented, as well as creative and business savvy. They must be able to provide leadership in a kitchen, because they're responsible for overseeing the entire operation, from cleanliness, health and safety issues down to ordering food and supplies, planning menus, creating recipes, measuring portion sizes and overseeing personnel. As the industry grows, foodworkers are being found in numerous places besides traditional restaurants, which can include such settings as grocery stores, private homes, nursing homes, schools and other areas where food is prepared and served. If the baking and pastry arts field is of interest to you, look over the following resources from Study.com to explore training and employment possibilities.

Education and Training Information

In the food service industry, it is possible to advance to coveted chef positions without postsecondary training by starting in an entry-level position in a restaurant, hotel or other venue and apprenticing under an experienced chef. There, one can learn the basics of running a kitchen. However, it is also possible to start preparing with formal training in the pastry chef specialty in several types of schools, such as culinary arts schools, community colleges or technical schools. After learning the basics of the industry, one who specializes in baking and pastry making can pursue one of four levels of pastry certification from the American Culinary Federation (www.acfchefs.org).
